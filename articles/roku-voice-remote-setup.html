How to set up and pair your Roku voice remote

This article walks you through putting batteries in your Roku voice remote, pairing it with your Roku device, and what to do if it ever becomes unpaired.

Before you start

  • Your Roku streaming device is plugged in and turned on.
  • Your TV is on and set to the correct HDMI input for the Roku device.
  • You have new or fully charged batteries for the remote.

1. Insert the batteries

  1. Slide open the battery cover on the back of the remote.
  2. Insert the batteries, matching the plus (+) and minus (−) symbols.
  3. Close the battery cover until it clicks into place.
Note: Some voice remotes use AA batteries and others use AAA. Check the marking inside the battery compartment.

2. Let the remote auto-pair (most devices)

On many Roku devices, the voice remote will try to pair automatically as soon as you insert the batteries and the device is powered on.

  1. Make sure your Roku home screen is visible on the TV.
  2. Wait a few seconds while the remote searches for your Roku device.
  3. If a pairing message appears on the screen, follow any prompts until pairing completes.

3. Manually start pairing

If the remote does not pair by itself, you can start pairing manually using the pairing button.

  1. Remove the battery cover to locate the pairing button.
  2. Press and hold the pairing button for about 5 seconds.
  3. Watch for the pairing light inside the battery compartment to begin flashing.
  4. After a few moments, you should see a message on the TV that the remote is pairing.

4. Check the pairing light and on-screen messages

  • Flashing light: The remote is in pairing mode and searching for your device.
  • No light: Check the batteries and try again.
  • On-screen success message: The remote is connected and ready to use.

5. Re-pairing a remote that stopped working

If your voice remote used to work but no longer controls the Roku device, try these steps:

  1. Unplug the power from your Roku device for 10 seconds, then plug it back in.
  2. Wait for the Roku home screen to appear.
  3. Remove the remote batteries and insert them again, or put in fresh batteries.
  4. Press and hold the pairing button for 5 seconds until the light starts flashing.
  5. Wait for the on-screen confirmation that pairing is complete.

6. If you still have issues

  • Move closer to the Roku device and remove anything blocking the signal path.
  • Reboot your Wi-Fi router if the remote uses a wireless (Wi-Fi Direct) connection.
  • Try another set of new batteries.
  • Visit the official Roku support article for more details: How to set up and pair your Roku voice remote .
