Which Roku remote do you have?

Roku offers multiple types of remotes — some with basic functions, others with voice control or extra features. This guide will help you identify which type you own, and what features you can expect.

Common Roku Remote Types

Simple / Infrared (IR) Remote

  • No pairing button under the battery cover.
  • Works by pointing directly at your streaming device or Roku TV — like a traditional TV remote.
  • Controls basic functions like navigation, Home, Back, etc.

Standard Roku Voice Remote

  • Has a pairing button inside or under the battery compartment — used to sync with your Roku device.
  • Uses wireless communication (RF, Bluetooth, or Wi-Fi Direct), not IR — so you don’t need to point directly at the Roku device. How to Tell What You Have

  • Open the back — if there’s no pairing button and no microphone hole, it's likely a simple IR remote.
  • If there’s a pairing button and a microphone (or a button with a mic icon), it’s likely a Voice Remote.
  • If the remote charges (e.g. via USB or docking) instead of using disposable batteries — that’s likely an Enhanced/Pro remote.
Note: Some Roku devices support both IR and wireless remotes, so even if your remote is wireless, your Roku player may accept IR remotes too — but some features (like voice or remote-finder) will only work with wireless remotes.

Why It Matters

  • Different remote types offer different features — knowing which you have helps when troubleshooting or replacing it.
  • If you lose your remote, you’ll know what type to order for full compatibility.
  • Wireless remotes (voice/enhanced) often support extra features such as voice search, TV volume & power, remote-finder, and sometimes pairing with multiple Roku devices — but need pairing to work. IR remotes are simpler but less powerful.
